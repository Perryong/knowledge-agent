## Executive Summary

All four positions were caught in the **June 5 "chips sector bloodbath"** — a $1T+ market-cap wipeout triggered by Broadcom guidance weakness and China chip export concerns around NVDA. NVDA dropped 6.2%, SOXL was decimated -30.5% (3x amplification), ASTS fell -12.8% amid SpaceX IPO hype cooling, and NOW gave back a month of recovery gains. For the week of June 9–13, the macro overhang (China chip policy, potential Senate hearings, enterprise AI disruption fears) creates a headwind-heavy environment. The most actionable setups are a **NVDA mean-reversion bounce** (if China news doesn't escalate) and **NOW stabilization** (AI accelerant narrative winning). ASTS and SOXL carry the highest risk.

---

## Market Context — What Happened June 5, 2026

The sector-wide selloff was driven by a convergence of three events:

1. **Broadcom guidance weakness** triggered the initial chip-sector dump
2. **NVDA-specific China risk** — Trump officials flagged a loophole allowing Chinese firms to buy Blackwell chips; Senator Elizabeth Warren called for Jensen Huang to testify; IBD reported backdoor AI chip sales to China
3. **SpaceX IPO hype cooling** — space sector sold off as SpaceX IPO enthusiasm faded following Blue Origin rocket issues; ASTS and peers were collateral damage

The Nasdaq fell 4.2% on the day. This was not a broad market meltdown — it was a concentrated semiconductor and AI narrative reset.
